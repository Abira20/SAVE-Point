Kepler’s 3rd Law

After years of studying data about the movements of planets and other objects in space, Johannes Kepler was able to identify a relationship between the time it takes a small object to complete an orbit and size of its orbit.  The basic pattern he noticed was that the farther an orbiting object was from the central source of gravity, the longer it took to complete one orbit.

        

The really cool part is that Kepler didn’t just notice this pattern, but he figured out exactly how to predict how fast an object would go.  The relationship between an orbiting object’s distance and the time it takes to orbit is more complicated than you might expect.  Objects in orbit don’t just increase their time to orbit by the same amount as they move out from the center.  For example, if you double the size of the orbit, the object takes more than twice as long to go around.
