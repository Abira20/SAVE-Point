Kepler’s 2nd Law:

Johannes Kepler was able to describe the speeds of objects as they traveled along their orbits. He noticed that objects moved at high speeds when they were close to the large central object, and they moved at low speeds when they were farther away.

Not only did he notice this general pattern, but he was able to mathematically calculate the speed of an object at any point along its orbit. He described this pattern by showing that the area swept out by an object is equal at all points along its orbit. This results in an object close to the central object moving faster to cover more distance in the same amount of time as an object farther away, which will move more slowly.
